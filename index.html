As an LMHC Candidate, my practice is fundamentally centered on Attachment Theory. I believe that most of our distress and relationship difficulties stem from a fear of disconnection. My role is to help you map the emotional bonds in your life—whether with yourself, a partner, or your family—and transform insecure patterns into secure, thriving connections.

I am highly trained in EFCT, a deeply effective, evidence-based modality. EFCT doesn't just manage symptoms; it targets the root cause of relational conflict by helping partners identify their negative interaction cycles and express their deeper, often vulnerable, emotional needs. This process creates a lasting, emotionally corrective experience that rewires the relationship for security.

Finding a therapist who truly understands your lived experience is essential. As a queer-affirming professional, I provide a non-pathologizing, celebratory space for all genders, sexual orientations, and relationship configurations (including polyamory and non-monogamy). You will never have to educate me on your identity or fight for validation here. Your intersectionality is honored and woven into our work together.

I look forward to helping you move from feeling stuck and isolated to experiencing authentic connection, resilience, and joy.

Services

Couple's Therapy (EFCT)

Focus: Breaking Negative Cycles & Emotional Repair

Are you stuck in the same arguments, feeling distant, or caught in a painful cycle of criticism and withdrawal? Using Emotionally Focused Couple's Therapy (EFCT), we’ll identify the root cause of your conflict—the fear of disconnection—and create new patterns of emotional safety. This process helps partners turn toward each other with vulnerability, leading to lasting repair and a secure, loving bond. Affirming of all relationship structures, including polyamory.

Individual Therapy

Focus: Trauma Healing & Self-Security

Your current struggles—whether anxiety, depression, or difficulty setting boundaries—are often linked to the emotional blueprints created in your past relationships. In individual therapy, we use an attachment lens to explore these patterns, heal old wounds, and establish a deeply rooted sense of self-security. This is a place for trauma recovery, authentic identity affirmation, and building powerful internal resilience.

Adolescent (Teen) Therapy

Focus: Identity, Family Systems, and Stress

Adolescence is a pivotal time for identity formation, and teens need a space to feel seen and understood without judgment. My work is grounded in family attachment, helping them navigate peer pressure, family conflict, academic stress, and gender or sexual identity discovery. I partner with parents to improve communication while providing the teen a confidential, empowering space to manage overwhelming feelings and grow.

Relationship Conflict Resolution

Focus: Communication & Collaborative Problem-Solving

Conflict doesn't have to mean crisis. This service is designed for any relational dyad—co-parents, family members, or business partners—who need to move past a repetitive pattern of antagonism. We focus on teaching practical communication skills, fostering empathy, and creating structural changes that allow participants to listen to needs instead of reacting to demands. The goal is collaborative, effective problem-solving and systemic harmony.

Frequently Asked Questions

Attachment-Focused Therapy looks at how your early life experiences shaped your emotional responses and relationship patterns. We focus on identifying these attachment styles (secure, anxious, avoidant) and work together to heal old wounds. The goal is to establish a more secure internal foundation, allowing you to connect authentically with yourself and others.

EFCT is highly effective for couples caught in repetitive conflict or emotional distance. It is an evidence-based method that helps partners break out of destructive cycles by accessing and communicating their deeper emotional needs (like feeling safe, loved, or valued). If you are looking for lasting repair and genuine intimacy, EFCT is an excellent fit. It works for all committed relationships.

'Queer-Affirming' means that I approach all sexual orientations, gender identities, and relationship structures (including Kink and CNM/Polyamory) as inherently valid and healthy. You will not spend time justifying your identity. Instead, we focus on the unique challenges you face—such as navigating chosen family, minority stress, and identity development—from a place of deep respect and celebration.

My full self-pay rate is competitive for the Albuquerque area. Crucially, I offer a sliding scale based on financial need to ensure therapy is accessible. Please contact me directly to discuss your income level and determine a sustainable rate for our work together. For insurance details, please refer to the Insurance/Payment section.

The first session is primarily an assessment phase. We will spend time reviewing the intake paperwork, clarifying your confidentiality rights, and, most importantly, discussing what brought you to therapy. I will ask questions about your history, your current distress, and what you hope to achieve. This is a collaborative session to ensure we are a good therapeutic fit before starting deeper work.

I offer both in-person sessions at my office in Albuquerque, NM, and HIPAA-compliant virtual (telehealth) sessions. Virtual sessions are available to anyone residing anywhere in the state of New Mexico. When booking your consultation, you can select the format that works best for your needs and location.
